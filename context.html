Context


To fully understand the context of the events that precede the Iliad there are multiple prophecies that have been laid out that explain characters and their stance. The main prophecies to know are:

꩜

Without Achilles to fight, the Greeks are prophesied to lose the war.

꩜

Paris will be the cause of the ultimate downfall of Troy.

꩜

Achilles is destined to either; live a long life in obscurity or die on the battlefield with his name known in glory and his life be retold for years to come.

The beginning of the knowledge you need to know before reading The Iliad is why the two cities are at war, and why the characters are actually fighting. The events differ slightly depending on their source but the main points are as follows.

Peleus and Thetis’ Wedding


Eris, the goddess of discord, was turned away at the door. Angered, she threw her wedding gift on the floor. A golden apple inscribed “for the fairest”. Three goddesses read the apple's words and claimed their gift, those being Hera, Athena, and Aphrodite.

Judgement of Paris


Since nobody could come to a decision. The three asked Paris to choose, who had been living in the countryside unaware of his ancestry following the prophecy that he would cause the downfall of Troy. The goddesses presented him with gifts.

Hera offered him political power. Athena offered him amounts of wisdom. Aphrodite offered him the love of the most beautiful woman in the world.

Helen's Abduction


Paris chose Aphrodite, who brought him Helen. Eros (Cupid) shot Helen and caused her to fall deeply in love with Paris. In response, Menelaus, Helen's husband, began planning war on Troy after being rejected to settle diplomatically.

This is all the main context you need to know to go into the Iliad with enough knowledge to follow along what is happening.
