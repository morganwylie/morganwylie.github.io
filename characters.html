Achaeans


Achilles


The central character in The Iliad. King of the Myrmidons and the most powerful warrior. His mother dipped him in the River Styx and he was made immortal apart from heel, this is where the term ‘Achilles’ Heel’ comes from. Achilles is hot-headed and easy to anger, proven with how he is brought to violence in the very first book.

Agamemnon


King of Mycenae. Agamemnon leads the Achaean army. Wealthy and Arrogant, Agamemnon’s leadership choices are questionable. He is crude and tactless.

Patroclus


Known mostly as Achilles' closest friend and lover in lots of translations and adaptations. He acts as an advisor for the other with a level-head, however he is ultimately slain by Hector after wearing Achilles’ armor to inspire the Greeks.

Odysseus


The protagonist of Homer’s The Odyssey, Odysseus is known as the cunning King of Ithaca. Odysseus and Nestor are regarded as the persuasive and tactful speakers of the Achaean army.

Diomedes


King of Argos and a major fighter in the war. Diomedes is inspired by Athena and injures Ares and Aphrodite in Book 5.

Great Ajax


One of the strongest Achaeans in the battle, Great Ajax is a commander who duels against Hector many times during the Iliad.

Little Ajax


Complemented by Great Ajax, little Ajax acts as his swift and nimble fighting partner. Although a common confusion, Great and Little Ajax are not brothers.

Nestor


The oldest commander for the Achaeans. Alongside Odysseus the two are advisors and Nestor is known for his wisdom from old age.

Menelaus


King of Sparta and brother of Agamemnon. The abduction of his wife Helen is the cause of the Trojan war.

Hera


Hera is the Queen of the Gods, and is known as Zeus’ wife. Harbouring a great disdain to the Trojans, Hera often teams up with Athena to aid the Achaeans.

Athena


Also hating the Trojans, Athena often aids the Achaeans, inspiring most notably Achilles, Odysseus, and Diomedes.

Poseidon


While being a much more influential figure in The Odyssey, Poseidon sides with the Achaens because of his grudge against the Trojans.

Hephaestus


Not referenced much, Hephestus aids the Greeks by forging Achilles strong armour to fight the Trojans after Patroclus is killed by Hector.

Trojans


Hector


A widely respected and skilled warrior, Hector is the elder prince of Troy and holds a contempt for his brother Paris for causing a war on their city. During Book 6 we see Hector’s devotion to his wife Andromache, and his efforts to ensure his son, Astyanax lives a glorious life.

Priam


King of Troy. Respected by both sides for his long life as a soldier, and is welcoming to Helen despite his anger at the war. Priam goes to retrieve Hector's body alone and grief stricken.

Paris


One of many prince’s of Troy, Paris is held responsible for the abduction of Helen and hence the Trojan war. He is cowardly and is content to sit in his room with Helen while the Trojans fight for him. However he joins Hector at the end of Book 6 in a wave of inspiration to fight.

Helen


The wife of Menelaus who has been abducted by Paris for being known as the most beautiful woman in the world. Helen struggles with her self-hatred at the guilt of being the cause of so much suffering from both Achaeans and Trojans.

Andromache


The mother of Astyanax and devoted wife of Hector.

Astyanax


The son of Andromache who is killed by either Neoptolemus or Odysseus after being thrown off the walls of Troy to avoid the future of him avenging his fathers death.

Chryses


Father of Chryseis and loyal priest of Apollo. When Chryseis is taken by Agamemnon and he refuses to return her to her father, Chryses calls for Apollo, who shoots an arrow of disease through the Achaean ships in the first book.

Briseis


One of the many women which Achilles keeps as a prize for his victories. Briseis is taken by Agamemnon after he is forced to return Chryseis, which causes Achilles’ hatred of the King to begin and his retaliation and refusal to fight.

Zeus


Claiming neutrality for most of the war. However after Agamemnon upsets Achilles, Thetis asks Zeus to help the Trojans per Achilles request.

Thetis


The prophecy that tells her son will die in the battle, Thetis attempts to aid the Trojans to keep her son alive.

Apollo


Known as the god of the sun and specifically music, Apollo heavily favours the Trojans and frequently aids them alongside Aphrodite.

Aphrodite


Paris, choosing Aphrodite, is the main reason of Aphrodites allegiance to the Trojans. She frequently interferes to help the Trojans, much to the mortals and gods dismay. Most notably she whisks Paris away when he is hurt in a duel between himself and Menelaus.
